# Humanize

Strip AI writing patterns from prose and restore natural voice.

## Core Rules

1. **Cut filler openers.** Most first sentences announce content instead of delivering it. Remove throat-clearing phrases. Start with the actual point. See [references/phrases.md](references/phrases.md).

2. **Replace AI vocabulary.** Certain words appear disproportionately in AI-generated text because models over-sample them. Swap them for concrete, specific alternatives. See [references/words.md](references/words.md).

3. **Break formulaic structure.** AI defaults to recognizable patterns: binary contrasts, dramatic sentence fragments, tricolons, rhetorical questions with obvious answers. Identify and dismantle them. See [references/structures.md](references/structures.md).

4. **Vary rhythm.** Three consecutive sentences of similar length sound metronomic. Mix short and long. Two items in a list are often stronger than three. Not every paragraph should end with a punchy one-liner.

5. **Trust the reader.** Humans don't explain their metaphors, preface every point with "it's important to note," or grant readers permission to feel things. State the point and move on.

6. **Pass the read-aloud test.** Read the text out loud. If you'd never say it that way in a conversation — even a professional one — rewrite it. Contractions, informal connectors, and varied sentence openings all help here.

## Quick Checks

Before delivering revised prose:

- Three sentences in a row the same length? Break one up.
- Paragraph ends with a one-liner punchline? Consider a longer ending instead.
- Em-dash before a reveal? Replace with a period or comma.
- Explaining a metaphor right after using it? Cut the explanation.
- Does the first sentence actually say anything? If not, delete it.
- Would you say this out loud? If not, rewrite it.

## Scoring

After editing, rate the text 1–10 on each dimension:

| Dimension | Question to ask |
|-----------|-----------------|
| Directness | Does it state facts, or announce them? |
| Rhythm | Are sentences varied in length and structure? |
| Trust | Does it respect reader intelligence? |
| Voice | Does it sound like a person wrote it? |
| Density | Is anything cuttable without losing meaning? |

Below 35/50: revise again.

## What Good Looks Like

Good humanized prose:
- Starts mid-thought, not with an announcement
- Uses specific words instead of general ones ("dropped 40%" not "declined significantly")
- Mixes sentence lengths without pattern
- Makes claims without pre-justifying them
- Doesn't end with a summary of what was just said
